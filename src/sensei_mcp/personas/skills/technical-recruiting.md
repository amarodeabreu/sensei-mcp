## 0. Core Principles (The Recruiting Way)

1.  **Hiring is a System, Not a Series of Events**
    One-off job posts don't scale. Build a repeatable recruiting engine.

2.  **Speed Wins**
    The best candidates are off the market in 7-14 days. Slow processes lose great people.

3.  **Candidate Experience = Employer Brand**
    Every candidate (hired or not) talks about your interview. Make it great.

4.  **Diversity is a Pipeline Problem, Not a Filter Problem**
    You can't hire diverse teams from a homogeneous pipeline. Source inclusively.

5.  **Interviewing is a Skill**
    Untrained interviewers make bad hiring decisions. Train your team.

6.  **Data Beats Intuition**
    Track conversion rates, time-to-hire, and source effectiveness. Optimize ruthlessly.

7.  **Compensation is a Competitive Sport**
    Underpay = lose candidates. Overpay = resentment. Be market-aware and fair.

8.  **Culture Add > Culture Fit**
    "Culture fit" often means "looks like us." Hire for diverse perspectives.

9.  **Sell the Mission, Not Just the Job**
    Engineers join companies for impact, learning, and team qualityâ€”not just salary.

10. **Hire for Slope, Not Intercept**
    Potential and learning velocity matter more than current skill level (especially for juniors).

### 2.4 Interview Process Design

**Goal:** Hire great engineers *fast* while minimizing false positives/negatives.

**Typical Funnel:**

```
100 Applications
  â†“ (Recruiter Screen: 40% pass)
40 Recruiter Screens
  â†“ (Technical Screen: 50% pass)
20 Technical Screens
  â†“ (Onsite: 40% pass)
8 Onsites
  â†“ (Offer: 100% make offer)
8 Offers
  â†“ (Acceptance: 70%)
5.6 Hires
```

**Conversion Optimization:**

-   **Low recruiter screen pass rate (<30%):** Job description is too broad, or pipeline quality is poor
-   **Low technical screen pass rate (<40%):** Bar is too high, or sourcing is off-target
-   **Low onsite pass rate (<30%):** Too many false positives in earlier stages
-   **Low offer acceptance (<60%):** Compensation, speed, or candidate experience issues

**Ideal Interview Stages:**

**Stage 1: Recruiter Screen (30 min)**

-   Assess basic qualifications (years of experience, tech stack match)
-   Sell the role and company (mission, team, growth)
-   Set expectations (process, timeline)
-   Output: Yes/No (40-50% pass rate)

**Stage 2: Technical Screen (45-60 min)**

-   Coding problem (LeetCode Easy-Medium) OR system design (for senior)
-   Assess: Problem-solving, code quality, communication
-   Platform: CoderPad, HackerRank, or live coding
-   Output: Yes/No (50% pass rate)

**Stage 3: Onsite (4-5 hours, 4-5 interviews)**

-   **Interview 1: Coding (60 min)** - LeetCode Medium-Hard
-   **Interview 2: System Design (60 min)** - Real-world problem (for mid/senior)
-   **Interview 3: Behavioral (45 min)** - STAR method, culture add
-   **Interview 4: Domain-Specific (60 min)** - Frontend (React), Backend (databases), ML (algorithms)
-   **Interview 5: Hiring Manager (30 min)** - Sell the role, answer questions
-   Output: Hire / No Hire (30-40% pass rate)

**Stage 4: Offer**

-   Present offer within 24-48 hours of onsite
-   Sell the opportunity (team, mission, growth)
-   Address concerns (compensation, title, location)
-   Output: Accept / Decline (70%+ acceptance rate)

**Interview Time Budget:**

-   Total candidate time: ~6-7 hours (screen + onsite)
-   Total company time: ~10-12 hours (4-5 interviewers Ã— 1-1.5 hours each)

**Speed Targets:**

-   Recruiter screen â†’ Technical screen: 3-5 days
-   Technical screen â†’ Onsite: 5-7 days
-   Onsite â†’ Offer: 1-2 days
-   Offer â†’ Decision: 3-5 days
-   **Total: 14-21 days** (industry best-in-class)

### 2.5 Interview Training (Calibration)

**Why Training Matters:**

-   Untrained interviewers = inconsistent evaluations
-   Bias (unconscious or conscious) = bad hiring decisions
-   Poor candidate experience = damaged brand

**Interviewer Training Curriculum:**

1.  **Unconscious Bias Training (1 hour)**
    -   Recognize affinity bias, halo effect, confirmation bias
    -   Use structured rubrics, not "gut feel"

2.  **Behavioral Interviewing (STAR method) (1 hour)**
    -   Situation, Task, Action, Result
    -   Avoid yes/no questions ("Tell me about a time when...")

3.  **Technical Interviewing Best Practices (2 hours)**
    -   Give candidates a real problem (not trivia)
    -   Allow them to ask clarifying questions
    -   Focus on thought process, not memorized solutions
    -   Provide hints if stuck (it's collaborative, not adversarial)

4.  **Calibration Sessions (Quarterly)**
    -   Review past interviews (anonymized)
    -   Discuss: "Would you hire this person? Why or why not?"
    -   Align on standards (what's a 'Hire' vs 'Strong Hire'?)

**Interviewer Scorecard (Standardized):**

| Criterion | Weight | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Problem Solving | 30% | 4 | Solved problem with hints |
| Code Quality | 20% | 3 | Readable but not optimized |
| Communication | 20% | 5 | Explained thought process clearly |
| Culture Add | 15% | 4 | Collaborative, asked good questions |
| Domain Knowledge | 15% | 3 | Adequate for level |
| **Weighted Score** | 100% | **3.8** | **Lean Hire** |

**Hiring Decision:**

-   **4.5-5.0:** Strong Hire
-   **3.5-4.4:** Hire
-   **2.5-3.4:** No Hire
-   **<2.5:** Strong No Hire

### 2.6 Offer Strategy & Compensation

**Compensation Philosophy:**

-   **Market Rate:** Pay at 50th-75th percentile (use levels.fyi, Pave, Radford)
-   **Location:** Adjust for cost-of-living (SF vs Austin vs Remote)
-   **Equity:** Early-stage = higher equity, late-stage = higher cash
-   **Leveling:** Senior Engineer in SF = $180-220K base + equity

**Offer Components:**

| Component | Example (Senior Engineer, SF) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Base Salary** | $200K |
| **Equity (RSUs or Options)** | $80K/year (4-year vest) |
| **Sign-On Bonus** | $20K (1-year clawback) |
| **Annual Bonus** | 10% of base ($20K) |
| **Total Comp (Year 1)** | **$320K** |

**Offer Negotiation Tactics:**

| Candidate Ask | Response Strategy |
|---------------|-------------------|
| "Can you go higher on base?" | "We're at market rate. Can we add equity or sign-on?" |
| "Competitor X offered $50K more" | "Can you share their offer? We'll match or beat on total comp." |
| "I need more time to decide" | "What's your timeline? Can we answer any questions?" |
| "I'm concerned about equity value" | "Here's our valuation, growth trajectory, and exit timeline." |

**Offer Acceptance Rate Optimization:**

-   **Speed:** Present offer within 48 hours of onsite (candidates lose interest if delayed)
-   **Sell the Role:** Hiring manager follow-up call ("We'd love to have you. Here's why.")
-   **Address Concerns:** Benefits, WLB, growth opportunities
-   **Compete on Total Comp:** Cash + equity + benefits + growth

### 2.7 Diversity & Inclusion in Hiring

**Why Diversity Matters:**

-   Diverse teams = better products (broader perspectives)
-   Diverse teams = better decision-making (research-backed)
-   Diverse teams = better retention (inclusive culture)

**Common Pitfalls:**

-   **Pipeline Excuse:** "We'd love to hire diverse candidates, but there aren't any in our pipeline."
    -   **Fix:** Source from diverse communities (Grace Hopper, AfroTech, Lesbians Who Tech)
-   **Culture Fit Bias:** "They don't feel like a fit" often means "They're different from us."
    -   **Fix:** Hire for "culture add," not "culture fit"
-   **Bar-Lowering Fear:** "We can't lower the bar just to hit diversity goals."
    -   **Fix:** Diversity doesn't mean lower bar. It means broader sourcing and bias-free evaluation.

**Diversity Hiring Strategy:**

1.  **Pipeline Diversification:**
    -   Partner with HBCUs (Howard, Spelman, Morehouse)
    -   Recruit at diversity conferences (Grace Hopper, Out in Tech)
    -   Sponsor coding bootcamps (Hack Reactor, Ada Developers Academy)

2.  **Bias-Free Process:**
    -   Blind resume reviews (remove names, schools, photos)
    -   Structured interviews (same questions for all candidates)
    -   Diverse interview panels (don't make one woman interview all women candidates)

3.  **Inclusive Job Descriptions:**
    -   Avoid gendered language ("rockstar," "ninja")
    -   Emphasize growth and learning (not just "10 years experience")
    -   Highlight diversity commitment ("We're an equal opportunity employer")

4.  **Retention:**
    -   Diverse hiring without inclusive culture = revolving door
    -   ERGs (Employee Resource Groups): Women in Tech, Black in Tech, LGBTQ+ in Tech
    -   Mentorship and sponsorship programs

## 5. Retention as Part of Recruiting

**Why Retention Matters:**

-   Losing a senior engineer = $200K+ replacement cost (recruiting + ramp-up)
-   High attrition = damaged employer brand
-   "Revolving door" culture = recruiting treadmill

**Retention Strategies:**

1.  **Competitive Compensation:** Annual raises, equity refreshes
2.  **Career Growth:** Clear promotion paths, mentorship
3.  **Challenging Work:** Avoid "boredom attrition" (give impactful projects)
4.  **Work-Life Balance:** Flexible hours, remote options, no burnout culture
5.  **Culture:** Inclusive, psychologically safe, collaborative

**Retention Metrics:**

| Cohort | 1-Year Retention | 2-Year Retention | Voluntary Attrition |
|--------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| 2022 Hires | 92% | 85% | 8% |
| 2023 Hires | 88% | TBD | 12% |
| 2024 Hires | 95% | TBD | 5% |

**Red Flags:**

-   Attrition >15%/year = culture or compensation problem
-   New hire attrition (0-6 months) >10% = onboarding or role mismatch
